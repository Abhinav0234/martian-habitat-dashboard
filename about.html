Aesthetic Choices and Reasoning

Dark Theme for Power Saving

The dashboard uses a pure black background (#000000) to minimize power consumption on OLED or E-INK panels, ideal for space habitats with limited energy. Subtle gradients and low-opacity overlays ensure readability without bright pixels.

Atkinson Hyperlegible Font

Chosen for exceptional readability, especially for astronauts with degraded vision or visors. Designed for accessibility, it ensures clarity in low-light space environments. Its unique letterforms reduce confusion, conveying a reliable, futuristic aesthetic for Martian habitats.

Color Palette

Blues (#00d4ff) for headers, evoking icy Mars. Reds (#ff6b6b) for alerts, signaling danger. Greens (#4ecdc4) for positives. Desaturated for eye strain reduction and power efficiency.

Glass Morphism Effects

Backdrop blur and transparencies create depth, mimicking sci-fi interfaces. Lightweight for space hardware, adding visual interest without heavy graphics. This design easily adapts to HUDs (Heads-Up Displays), allowing astronauts to overlay the dashboard on visors or AR glasses for on-the-go monitoring without obstructing their view of the environment.

Animations and Transitions

Subtle hovers and alert pulses enhance UX. CSS-based for efficiency, avoiding JS overhead in resource-constrained environments.

Layout

Grid-based responsive design for tablets to monitors. Sidebar for metrics, main for details, ensuring usability in habitat control rooms.

Implementation Context

This dashboard is the CS track submission for the Divergent Teams DC Hackathon, simulating life-support management in Martian/Lunar habitats for 4-6 users.
